Design a polished, startup-grade web app called “LawWise” — an AI legal assistant for India. This should feel trustworthy, modern, premium, and serious, not like a generic AI chatbot or a hackathon MVP. The visual style should combine legal credibility, civic clarity, and strong product design. Avoid flashy AI aesthetics, purple gradients, toy-like illustrations, or overused chatbot layouts. The product should look like a real venture-backed legal-tech company.

Create a desktop-first responsive web app with a refined information architecture, clear hierarchy, and a calm, professional visual language. Use a warm light background, strong typography, clean spacing, subtle depth, and restrained color accents such as deep navy, ink black, muted teal, sandstone, and soft gold. The UI should feel intentional and editorial, with premium cards, layered sections, soft shadows, structured grids, iconography, and excellent whitespace. Use modern startup product design, not government portal design, but keep the tone highly trustworthy and legally serious.

This app helps Indian users:
- understand legal rights
- ask legal questions in plain language
- upload documents like FIRs, notices, rental agreements
- find nearby lawyers, courts, police stations, and legal aid
- get step-by-step legal action plans
- switch between Indian languages
- access emergency legal help quickly

Design the following core screens and flows:

1. Landing Page
Create a premium homepage that clearly explains the product in a few seconds.
Sections:
- Hero section with headline like “Understand Your Legal Rights, Clearly.”
- Short supporting text focused on trust, clarity, citations, and actionability
- Primary CTA: “Ask LawWise”
- Secondary CTA: “Analyze a Document”
- Trust strip showing “Grounded in Indian legal sources”, “State-aware guidance”, “Multilingual support”, “Action plans with citations”
- Feature overview cards
- “How it works” 3-step section
- “For common situations” section with examples like police stop, tenant dispute, harassment complaint, traffic fine, workplace issue
- Footer with product links, legal disclaimer, privacy, contact

The homepage should feel like a real startup website with elegant section transitions, strong typography, and premium composition.

2. Login / Onboarding
Create a minimal, clean login and sign-up experience.
Include:
- Continue as guest option
- Sign in with email / Google
- Small explanation that users can save timeline, documents, and history if they sign in
- Language selector and location preference during onboarding
This should feel lightweight, not enterprise-heavy.

3. Main App Shell
Design the primary logged-in app layout.
Include:
- Left sidebar navigation
- Top bar with search, location selector, language selector, profile menu
- Persistent emergency help button visible but not intrusive
- Main workspace area with cards, sections, and conversation panel
Sidebar items:
- Ask LawWise
- Document Analysis
- Find Help
- My Timeline
- Emergency
- Settings

The app shell should feel like a serious SaaS product, not a simple chat page.

4. Ask LawWise Screen
This is the main legal assistant experience.
Design a sophisticated legal guidance workspace, not just chat bubbles.
Layout:
- Large central conversation area
- Right-side insights panel or contextual panel
- Starter prompts for common legal situations
- Follow-up question cards
- Citation cards below answers
- “What you can do next” action-plan panel
- “Evidence to collect” checklist
- “Urgency level” badge or risk panel
- “Need nearby help?” button linking to locator
The answer design should show:
- plain language explanation
- legal basis / source references
- step-by-step recommended actions
- uncertainty or caution notes when needed

Avoid making this look like ChatGPT. It should feel like a legal operations workspace.

5. Document Analysis Screen
Design a document upload and analysis interface.
Include:
- drag-and-drop upload zone
- supported files section
- recent uploads panel
- document preview area
- AI summary card
- risk flags card
- clause breakdown panel
- highlighted sections in the document
- actions like “simplify”, “flag risky terms”, “generate response draft”
This screen should feel like a premium legal document intelligence product.

6. Find Help Screen
Design a service locator page for legal help.
Include:
- search by case type and location
- map section
- nearby results list
- filters for lawyer, court, police station, legal aid center
- result cards with distance, type, contact, hours, and action buttons
- trusted/legal-aid badge where applicable
This screen should feel highly practical and location-aware.

7. My Timeline Screen
Design a case timeline / memory page.
Include:
- chronological record of past issues, actions taken, uploaded documents, and advice received
- expandable timeline cards
- status labels like “pending”, “action needed”, “resolved”
- reminders or next steps
This should feel structured and useful, not cluttered.

8. Emergency Screen
Design a high-priority legal emergency page.
This screen must be simple, fast, and static-feeling, with no unnecessary AI complexity.
Include:
- emergency call buttons
- legal helpline info
- police / women’s helpline / child helpline / legal aid quick actions
- nearest police station / hospital / legal aid quick access
- strong visual emphasis on urgency and clarity
This should feel direct, calm, and reliable.

9. Settings Screen
Design a neat settings page with sections for:
- language preferences
- location preferences
- privacy controls
- saved documents
- memory/history controls
- notifications
- dark mode optional but not the default
Keep it clean and product-focused.

Design system and interaction notes:
- Desktop-first, mobile-responsive
- Premium startup design, not template-like
- Use a clear visual hierarchy and strong typography
- Prefer editorial/product UI style over generic SaaS cards everywhere
- Avoid overusing rounded gradients or “AI assistant” clichés
- Keep layouts asymmetric where appropriate so it feels custom and designed
- Add subtle icons and visual separators
- Show trust through layout, labels, citations, and clarity
- Make room for multilingual support and Indian context
- Include meaningful empty states and starter states
- The app must feel legally serious, calm, human-centered, and highly usable

Overall mood:
A high-trust, venture-backed Indian legal-tech product with excellent UX, premium interface design, strong product thinking, and a carefully designed experience for real people facing legal problems.
